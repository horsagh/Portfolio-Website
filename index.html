HACKBOX

5/2023-Present

HACKBOX is a “minigame compilation” mobile game I designed and developed in Unity with another student, funded by a grant from the NSA. The Trials of Persephone

3/2022-Present

The Trials of Persephone is a first person spellslinger game developed in Unreal Engine 5. It's a personal project that I am developing with the help of a 3D artist. The idea started as a VR game, however in testing I found that the concept didn't play well to VR's strengths, so I remade it in first person.

Ruins

2/2019-4/2021

Ruins is a CS:GO bomb defusal level created in the hammer editor. It was a solo personal project. I designed and iterated on the layout for engaging and dynamic gameplay. I conducted multiple playtests and analyzed popular maps extensively. I detailed the map with assets and textures from other source levels.

Croak and Dagger

8/2022-Present

Croak and Dagger is a narrative driven hack n slash that is being designed and developed in Unity by myself and a team of 19 others over the course of 2 semesters. I worked on designing the combat system and levels, and developed some of the AI and VFX.

Steel Echoes

9/2023-12/2023

Steel Echoes is a mechbuilding Tabletop Roleplaying Game that I designed and developed along with two other students as an academic project. The Halls

1/2021-5/2021

The Halls is an endless topdown roguelike written in C# using MonoGame. It was an academic project that I worked on with 3 other students. I created the room sequencing system, AI, and player mechanics. I also implemented the UI, animations and VFX.

Clone!

4/2022-5/2022

Clone! is a web puzzle game written in JavaScript using PixiJS. It was a solo academic project. The game's core mechanic is cloning- you can use your clones as bait to move enemies into vulnerable positions. I developed and utilized multiple collision detection algorithims for this game, including AABB, circle-circle and a line trace function.

D&D Encounter Builder

4/2022

D&D Encounter Builder is a web based tool written in JavaScript for planning Dungeons & Dragons. I wrote it on my own using the Open 5e API, in about a week. It allows you to search for monsters with a number of attributes, and then select any number of them to fight your players. It then gives you an approximation for how the fight will turn out.

About

Hello! I am a game designer and programmer from Rochester, NY. I specialize in level design and AI and I am passionate about creating immersive, engaging, and memorable gaming experiences!

As a game designer, I am always looking to invent fresh and unique game mechanics, and I fully embrace the rapid prototyping methodology. I also excel at problem solving, and I’m never afraid to think outside the box when it comes to finding a solution. I believe the best way to make a game is to start with a clear goal and constantly have the player’s psychology in mind.

Outside of games, I love to stay active with ultimate frisbee, and when it’s too cold for that I love to snowboard. I also have a passion for worldbuilding and writing.
